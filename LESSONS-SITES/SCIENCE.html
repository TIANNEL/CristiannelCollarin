Computing and Other Sciences

Definitions

  • Computing: The use of technology to input and process data to produce a desired output applicable to any industry.
  • Science: A system of knowledge concerning the physical world and its phenomena, characterized by unbiased observations and systematic experimentation.

Computing and Formal Science

  • Key Fields: Mathematics, logic, statistics, cryptography, and robotics.
  • Methodology: Uses abstract logical systems (such as True/False values) that can be applied to other sciences via quantitative research.
  • Technological Benefits:
    • Calculators: Essential for mathematical computation.
    • Spreadsheets: Facilitate complex formulas and statistical graphing.
    • AI and Robotics: Combine mathematics and reasoning to advance research implementation.
    • Virtual Environments: Provide spaces for testing mathematical and logical ideas.

Computing and Natural Science

  • Key Fields: Biology, chemistry, physics, astronomy, and environmental science.
  • Methodology: Relies on quantifiable data, mathematical formulae, and rigorous peer review for validity and accuracy.
  • Technological Benefits:
    • Electron Microscopes: Allow biologists to inspect microscopic objects like viral loads.
    • CT Scans: Provide high-contrast X-ray images of sensitive internal organs.
    • Gamma Ray Telescopes: Mounted on satellites to observe distant celestial bodies.
    • Digital Sensors: Monitor movements and changes in the ocean.

Computing and Social Science

  • Key Fields: Anthropology, archaeology, psychology, sociology, and education.
  • Methodology: Uses both quantitative and qualitative research, though it focuses more heavily on qualitative data and eclectic social research.
  • Technological Benefits:
    • Online Forms: Facilitate the collection of census data and polls.
    • The Internet: Connects people across distances and provides educational websites.
    • Social Networks: Enable the observation of broader societal trends.
    • Hardware: Laptops and projectors serve as primary teaching tools in education.
