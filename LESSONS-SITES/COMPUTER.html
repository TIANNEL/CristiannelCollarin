The Computer System

Definitions

  • Computer System: A machine that accepts data (input), processes it according to stored instructions, and produces results (output).
  • Hardware vs. Software: Hardware refers to the tangible parts (CPU, RAM), while software refers to the intangible programs (OS, Applications).
  • Binary System: The fundamental language of computers using bits (0 for Off/False and 1 for On/True).
  • The Internet: A global system of interconnected computer networks communicating via the TCP/IP protocol suite.

Theoretical & Operational Foundations

  • Machine Cycle: The four-step process the CPU follows to execute instructions:
    • Fetch: Retrieve the instruction from memory.
    • Decode: Translate the instruction into computer commands.
    • Execute: Carry out the command.
    • Store: Write the result back to memory.
  • Logic Operations: Performed by the ALU, including arithmetic (+, -, *, /) and logical comparisons (AND, OR, NOT).

Categories and Signals

  • Computer Categories:
    • Microcomputers: Laptops, desktops, and smartphones for personal use.
    • Mainframes: Powerful systems used by large institutions like banks for bulk data processing.
    • Supercomputers: High-performance machines for scientific research and weather forecasting.
  • Signal Types: Information is transmitted via Analog (continuous) or Digital (discrete finite values) signals.

Key Figures & Pioneers

  • Charles Babbage: The "Father of Computing" who designed the Analytical Engine.
  • Alan Turing: Developed the theoretical basis for modern computers and artificial intelligence.
  • Grace Hopper: A pioneer in computer programming who developed COBOL, the first major English-like language.
  • Jobs & Wozniak: Co-founders of Apple who developed the first successful single-circuit board personal computer.
  • Tim Berners-Lee: The inventor of the World Wide Web (WWW) and HTML.
